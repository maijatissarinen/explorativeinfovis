What happens when sea levels rise?

13th sustainable development goal: Climate change

One major consequence of climate change, is the rising of sea levels. As European, the other effects of climate change, such as extreme weather conditions may not concern us personally, because they are often located in the continents of North and South America, as well as, Asia. Sea levels rising, however will concern us, as European countries are at great risk to suffer from the consequences of rising sea levels.

This visualization showcases a situation in which Europe faces the high emission scenario RCP 8.5 - SSP5, also known as the fossil fuel based development pathway. In this scenario, global economic growth relys heavily on fossil fuels, accelerating the global warming and sea-level rise.

The map visualizes the median expected annual population exposed from coastal flooding. This amount is expressed in thousand people.

Year: 2000
